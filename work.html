Escher's tessellations feature repetitive, geometric shapes which fit together in dazzling ways. Often inspired by nature and animals, Escher's tessellations are perhaps his most well-known works.

Another predominant subject in Escher's work are staircases, often oriented in eye-bending perspectives. As a boy, Escher was often banished to the hallway during classes and would have plenty of time to study and sketch the dramatic staircases in his school.

Escher often added his love of illusions and strange perspectives to his other subjects, including his portraits. Depicted here is his reflection in a smooth metal ball.

Having many scientist and mathematician friends, Escher had access to an inner circle making fantastic discoveries. He was fascinated by the graphical represntations of the laws of his friends' work.
